## Getting Around

Tokyo's public transit system is world-class. Get a Suica or Pasmo card at any station for seamless travel across trains, subways, and buses.

### Key Tips

- Avoid rush hour (7:30-9:30 AM, 5:30-8:00 PM)
- Google Maps works well for transit directions
- The JR Yamanote Line circles central Tokyo and connects major stations

## Neighborhoods

### Shibuya
Famous for the scramble crossing and youth culture. Great for shopping and nightlife.

### Shinjuku
Home to the busiest train station in the world. Don't miss Golden Gai for tiny bars and Omoide Yokocho for yakitori.

### Asakusa
Traditional Tokyo. Visit Senso-ji temple and explore the historic streets.

## Food Recommendations

- **Ramen**: Ichiran (customizable solo booths) or Fuunji (tsukemen)
- **Sushi**: Tsukiji Outer Market or any conveyor belt spot
- **Coffee**: Blue Bottle, Onibus, or Fuglen

## Day Trips

- **Nikko**: Temples and nature (2 hours)
- **Kamakura**: Great Buddha and hiking (1 hour)
- **Hakone**: Hot springs and Mt. Fuji views (1.5 hours)
